The Trends Researcher

Before this agent, I spent 3+ hours every week manually scanning LinkedIn, newsletters, and competitor feeds looking for content ideas worth pursuing. Now a Claude agent does it automatically, scores each trend against our ICP context, and posts a ranked list to Slack every Monday morning.

  • 3 hours per week back
  • Pulls from 40+ sources automatically
  • Scores each trend against your context graph
  • Drops a ranked Slack digest every Monday at 9am
Claude System Prompt (copy and customize)
You are a B2B content trends researcher for [COMPANY]. Your ICP: [describe your ideal customer profile in 2-3 sentences] Your content pillars: [list 3-5 topics you write about] Every week you will receive a batch of raw content signals pulled from newsletters, LinkedIn posts, and industry feeds. Your job is to: 1. Identify the 8-10 most relevant trends for our ICP 2. Score each trend from 1-10 on three dimensions: - ICP relevance (does our buyer care about this?) - Timeliness (is this peaking now or fading?) - Angle potential (can we say something non-obvious?) 3. Return a ranked list in this format: TREND: [topic name] SCORE: [X/30] WHY NOW: [1 sentence on timing] OUR ANGLE: [1 sentence on the non-obvious take] FORMAT SUGGESTION: [thread / carousel / short post / long post] Output only the ranked list. No preamble.

n8n Trigger Setup
Trigger: Schedule (every Monday 08:30) Step 1: HTTP Request nodes pulling RSS feeds from your 40+ sources Step 2: Aggregate all items from the past 7 days into one payload Step 3: Claude API node with the system prompt above Step 4: Slack node posting formatted digest to #content-ideas

The Executive Assistant

Meeting prep, follow-up emails, action item tracking, scheduling coordination. This is the invisible work that makes a founder productive but used to eat 10+ hours of my week. Now a Claude agent reads my calendar, pulls context from Attio and Slack, writes briefs before every call, and drafts follow-ups after.

  • 10+ hours per week back
  • Meeting brief generated 30 minutes before every call
  • Follow-up email drafted within 5 minutes of hanging up
  • Zero missed action items since March
Pre-Call Brief Prompt (copy and customize)
You are an executive assistant preparing a pre-call brief for [YOUR NAME]. You will receive: - Meeting title and attendee names - CRM notes pulled from Attio for each attendee - Recent Slack messages mentioning the company or contact - Last email thread with this person (if any) Generate a brief in this format: WHO THEY ARE [2-3 sentence summary of the company and the contact's role] WHERE WE LEFT OFF [What was discussed or agreed in the last interaction] CONTEXT SIGNALS [2-3 relevant signals from Slack/email/CRM that are worth knowing] SUGGESTED AGENDA [3 bullet points for how to structure the 30 minutes] WATCH OUT FOR [1-2 potential friction points or sensitivities based on history] Keep the brief under 250 words. Write in plain English, no jargon.

Post-Call Follow-Up Prompt
You are drafting a follow-up email after a sales or CS call. You will receive a call transcript or summary. Extract: 1. The 3 most important things discussed 2. Any commitments made (by either party) 3. The agreed next step and timeline Then draft a follow-up email in this format: - Subject: Re: [call topic] + next steps - Opening: 1 sentence acknowledging the conversation - Body: what we discussed (3 bullets max), what we each committed to - Close: confirm the next step with a specific date or deadline Write in a warm but professional tone. Do not use filler phrases like "As discussed" or "Per our conversation." No em dashes anywhere in the output.

n8n Trigger Setup
PRE-CALL WORKFLOW Trigger: Google Calendar event starts in 30 minutes Step 1: Fetch attendee emails from the event Step 2: Attio lookup by company domain for each attendee Step 3: Slack search for messages mentioning the company (last 14 days) Step 4: Claude API call with pre-call brief prompt Step 5: Slack DM to yourself with the brief POST-CALL WORKFLOW Trigger: Calendar event ends (or webhook from your meeting recorder) Step 1: Pull transcript from Granola / Fireflies / Fathom Step 2: Claude API call with follow-up prompt Step 3: Gmail draft created, labeled "AI Draft - Review Before Sending"

The Business Development Rep

Prospect research, personalized outreach drafts, pipeline updates. The agent reads our Attio CRM, identifies signals, and drafts outreach that references real context about the prospect's company and content. What used to take 45 minutes per prospect now takes 3 minutes.

  • 45 minutes of research per prospect compressed to 3 minutes
  • Personalization that references their actual LinkedIn posts
  • Pipeline notes updated automatically after every touch
  • Outreach quality is higher than what most humans produce at speed
Prospect Research + Outreach Prompt (copy and customize)
You are a BDR at [COMPANY] doing research on a new prospect before writing outreach. You will receive: - Company name, website, and LinkedIn URL - The prospect's job title and LinkedIn posts from the last 30 days - Any existing Attio CRM notes on this account Your ICP: [describe your target buyer in 2-3 sentences] Your value prop: [what problem you solve, in one sentence] Step 1 - Research summary: Write a 5-bullet summary of the company. Include: what they do, their GTM motion, likely team size for [relevant function], any recent announcements or hires, any signal that suggests they have the problem we solve. Step 2 - Outreach draft: Write a 4-line cold email. Line 1: Something specific about them (from their posts or news) that shows you did the work. Line 2: Connect it to the problem we solve. Line 3: Social proof (one relevant client or result). Line 4: Low-friction CTA (offer value, not a meeting). Do not use "I came across your profile." Do not use em dashes. Keep the whole email under 80 words.

n8n Trigger Setup
Trigger: Attio webhook when a new company is added to a specific list Step 1: Fetch company data from Attio (website, LinkedIn, notes) Step 2: Web scrape LinkedIn posts for the primary contact (Apify or PhantomBuster) Step 3: Claude API call with the research + outreach prompt Step 4: Write research summary back to Attio as a note Step 5: Post outreach draft to Slack #bdr-queue for human review before sending
The Stack

Everything You Need to Replicate This

The total stack costs approximately $20/month. Here is what you actually need and what you can skip at the start.

Orchestration

n8n (self-hosted)

Free on your own VPS. $20/month on Railway or Render. Handles all triggers and routing between tools.

AI Brain

Claude API

Anthropic API via n8n HTTP node. At the volume described here, costs roughly $5-10/month depending on usage.

CRM

Attio

Native webhooks and a clean API make it the best CRM for n8n integrations. Replaces HubSpot for most early-stage teams.

Comms

Slack + Gmail

Outputs route to Slack for team visibility and Gmail for draft reviews. Both have native n8n nodes.

Calendar

Google Calendar

Triggers the pre-call brief workflow. n8n polls for events starting in 30 minutes every 5 minutes.

Transcripts

Granola / Fathom

Either works for post-call transcript delivery. Granola has a direct MCP integration if you use Claude desktop.
